Greenhouse Gases

This lesson explores the role of greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere and how human activities are influencing climate change through increased emissions.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of greenhouse gases and their sources.
  • Explore the impact of greenhouse gases on global warming and climate change.
  • Examine how reducing emissions can mitigate climate change.

Key Concepts

Greenhouse Gases: Greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O), trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to the greenhouse effect and global warming.

Sources of Emissions: Major sources of greenhouse gas emissions include fossil fuel combustion, deforestation, and industrial activities.

  • Human activities have significantly increased the concentration of greenhouse gases.
  • The burning of fossil fuels for electricity, heat, and transportation is the largest source of greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Reducing emissions requires transitioning to renewable energy sources, enhancing energy efficiency, and protecting forests.

Case Study: Paris Climate Agreement

The Paris Climate Agreement is a global initiative to combat climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
It aims to limit global warming to below 2°C compared to pre-industrial levels.

Activity: Measure Your Carbon Footprint

Students will calculate their carbon footprint by examining their daily activities, such as transportation and energy use. They will explore ways to reduce their carbon footprint by adopting sustainable practices.
