Goal 14: Life Below Water

Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. Goal 14 focuses on ensuring the health and sustainability of marine ecosystems and reducing marine pollution.

Did You Know?

Oceans cover 70% of the Earthâ€™s surface, and more than 3 billion people depend on marine resources for their livelihoods. Goal 14 aims to reduce marine pollution and protect marine ecosystems from degradation.

Targets and Indicators

Target 14.1: Reduce Marine Pollution

By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, particularly from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution.

Indicator: Index of coastal eutrophication and floating plastic debris density.

Target 14.2: Protect Marine Ecosystems

Sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems to avoid significant adverse impacts, by strengthening their resilience and taking action for their restoration.

Indicator: Proportion of national exclusive economic zones managed using ecosystem-based approaches.

How You Can Take Action

Reduce Plastic Use

Minimize single-use plastic consumption and participate in coastal clean-up initiatives to help reduce marine debris.

Support Marine Conservation

Engage in or support marine conservation efforts through advocacy, donations, or volunteering for organizations that focus on protecting marine ecosystems.

Educate Others

Raise awareness about the importance of healthy oceans and marine life by educating your community and peers about the impact of marine pollution.
