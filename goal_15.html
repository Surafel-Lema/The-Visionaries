Goal 15: Life on Land

Protect, restore, and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation, and halt biodiversity loss.

Did You Know?

Forests are home to more than 80% of all terrestrial species of animals, plants, and fungi. Goal 15 focuses on halting deforestation and protecting ecosystems to prevent biodiversity loss.

Targets and Indicators

Target 15.1: Conserve Ecosystems

Ensure the conservation, restoration, and sustainable use of terrestrial and inland freshwater ecosystems and their services, particularly forests, wetlands, mountains, and drylands.

Indicator: Proportion of forest area, wetlands, and mountains conserved as part of national policy frameworks.

Target 15.2: Halt Deforestation

Promote the implementation of sustainable management of all types of forests, halt deforestation, restore degraded forests, and substantially increase afforestation and reforestation globally by 2030.

Indicator: Progress towards sustainable forest management, including forest cover and management certification rates.

How You Can Take Action

Plant Trees

Participate in local tree-planting initiatives to help restore forested areas and promote biodiversity.

Support Conservation

Engage in conservation efforts to protect endangered species and preserve habitats for wildlife.

Reduce Deforestation

Advocate for policies that limit deforestation, and reduce your carbon footprint by using products sourced from sustainably managed forests.
