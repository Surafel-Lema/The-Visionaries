Extreme Heat

This lesson focuses on understanding the causes and impacts of extreme heat events, exploring adaptation strategies, and recognizing the importance of resilience in our communities.

Learning Objectives

  • Define extreme heat and its effects on human health and the environment.
  • Identify adaptation strategies for communities affected by extreme heat.
  • Explore case studies demonstrating resilience against heat waves.

Key Concepts

Extreme Heat Events: These are significant increases in temperature, often leading to health risks, including heat stress and heat-related illnesses.

Resilience: Building resilience involves preparing for and adapting to the impacts of extreme heat through effective planning and community engagement.

  • Extreme heat disproportionately affects vulnerable populations, including the elderly and those with pre-existing health conditions.
  • Urban areas often experience the "urban heat island" effect, leading to higher temperatures.
  • Effective communication and public awareness are essential during extreme heat events.

Case Study: The Heat Wave of 2021

The 2021 heat wave in the Pacific Northwest showcased the devastating impacts of extreme heat on communities,
leading to increased hospitalizations and fatalities. This event highlighted the need for effective heat response plans.

Activity: Create a Heat Action Plan

Students will design a community heat action plan addressing how to prepare for and respond to extreme heat events. This includes identifying vulnerable populations and resources for support.
